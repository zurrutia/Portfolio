02
Edge Server Backup at a Bandwidth-Constrained Remote Site
Agilyx / Client Site (Toyo)
Edge Compute Remote Site Stratus ztC Edge Tosibox VPN OVF / VHD Export

The Problem

A 120GB historian VM running on a Stratus ztC Edge server at a remote industrial site needed a verified backup. The only connectivity was a Tosibox VPN over a cellular hotspot, effectively a few Mbps of usable bandwidth. Any network-based transfer would have taken days with a high probability of failure mid-stream.

Constraints

The Stratus platform does not support saving VM exports to its own local filesystem. A CIFS network share is required as the export destination. Routing that share over the Tosibox link would have placed the bandwidth bottleneck directly in the backup path, making things worse rather than better.

Decision Process

Evaluated three paths: a local LAN PC as a CIFS target (fast over LAN, requires physical retrieval), a remote Tosibox-connected PC as the target (ruled out, saturates the constrained link during the export job), and USB export directly from the Stratus console to an external drive. USB export was selected as the lowest-risk path. Documented the approach, risks, and workflow for operations and management before any work at the site began.

Forward-Deployed Relevance This is exactly the problem topology that comes up in field-deployed environments: edge hardware, degraded connectivity, and a need to make sound decisions about data integrity without a datacenter safety net. The ability to reason through it and communicate the tradeoffs clearly was as important as the technical answer.
